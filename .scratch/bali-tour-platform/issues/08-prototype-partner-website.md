# Prototype partner-facing website concept

Type: prototype
Status: resolved
Blocked by: none

Create a throwaway, shareable website mockup that makes the emerging Russian-market offer concrete enough for the Bali operating partner to review. It must expose unverified packages, prices, inclusions, trust claims, payment methods, and Russian copy as placeholders rather than facts.

## Question

Which information hierarchy and enquiry journey best communicates the business concept to the partner without implying that the offer is production-ready?

## Done when

- Three structurally distinct UI variants can be switched via `?variant=`.
- The prototype runs locally with one command and works on mobile and desktop.
- No competitor assets, copied text, customer data, fabricated reviews, or unsupported legal/payment claims are used.
- The partner can react to package hierarchy, trust information, and enquiry flow.

## Comments

- 2026-07-11: Created and claimed after the user clarified that a concrete mockup is needed now for partner alignment. This prototype informs discovery; it does not replace package, legal, content-rights, or payment validation.

## Resolution

Resolved on 2026-07-11. Erland selected Variant C as the primary direction because consultation and customer handling are his responsibility. The intended premium direction combines C's concierge journey with B's structured catalogue/comparison and A's trust/team proof. OpenBali is retained only as a category benchmark for visible pricing, route catalogue, benefits, vehicle information, and multi-channel contact; its visual identity, copy, photos, design, and assets must not be copied. The switcher remains temporarily available for partner review and must be removed when the selected direction is rewritten as a real implementation.
